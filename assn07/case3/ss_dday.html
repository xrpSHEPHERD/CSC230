The Normandy Invasion

The invasion on the beaches of Normandy on June 6, 1944, was the largest air, land, and sea operation undertaken before or since June 6, 1944. Operation Overlord involved over 150,000 servicemen stationed on over 5,000 ships and 11,000 airplanes. The initial wave of soldiers on the beaches had to cover over 200 yards of small-arms fire and artillery before reaching any protection. Casualties were immense: 4,000 men died on the beaches, and 6,000 were wounded.
